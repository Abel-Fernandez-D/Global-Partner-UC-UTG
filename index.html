Word Search Challenge: Financial Education

Click on a word in the list below to reveal a fun fact or statistic after finding it in the grid!

Words to Find

  • Saving Saving is the portion of income not spent on immediate consumption, set aside for future use. It helps individuals prepare for emergencies, achieve financial goals, or invest.
    Examples: In Peru, young people save using yape or cajas municipales. In The Gambia, osusu groups help youth save for school or business.
  • Tax A tax is a compulsory financial charge imposed by a government to fund public services.
    Examples: Peru uses IGV; in The Gambia, VAT applies to market goods.
  • Asset An asset is anything valuable that can be used to generate income or hold value over time.
    Examples: A laptop in Peru or a goat in The Gambia.
  • Liability A liability is a debt or obligation owed to others.
    Examples: Student loans in Peru; microfinance loans in The Gambia.
  • Balance A balance sheet shows what is owned, owed, and the net worth.
    Examples: Fondep funding in Peru or youth cooperatives in The Gambia.
  • Supply Supply refers to the quantity of goods producers are willing to offer.
    Examples: Avocado season in Peru; fish market in The Gambia.
  • Demand Demand is the quantity consumers are willing to buy.
    Examples: Mobile data in Peru; school uniforms in The Gambia.
  • Inflation Inflation is the rise in prices over time, reducing purchasing power.
    Examples: Price spikes in Peru and food affordability in The Gambia.
  • Warranty A warranty ensures product quality and allows returns or repairs.
    Examples: Phone purchases in Peru; electronics in The Gambia.
  • Complaint A complaint is a formal expression of dissatisfaction.
    Examples: Libro de Reclamaciones in Peru; market claims in The Gambia.
